Identify Your Blood Group

Identifying your blood group helps ensure safe medical transfusions and promotes general health awareness. Knowing your blood group can save lives during emergencies and help match donors with recipients.

Blood Group Types

A+

Has A antigens on the red blood cells and the Rh factor.

Compatible with A+, A-, AB+, and AB-

A-

Similar to A+, but lacks the Rh factor.

Compatible with A-, AB-, and O-

B+

Has B antigens and the Rh factor.

Compatible with B+, B-, AB+, and AB-

B-

Has B antigens but no Rh factor.

Compatible with B- and AB-

AB+

Universal recipient, has both A and B antigens and the Rh factor.

Can receive blood from any group

AB-

Has both A and B antigens but no Rh factor.

Can receive from AB-, A-, B-, and O-

O+

Has no A or B antigens but has the Rh factor.

Can donate to all positive blood types

O-

Universal donor, lacks A, B, and Rh antigens.

Can donate to any blood type
